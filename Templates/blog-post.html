Government Sets August 29, 2025 Deadline for New Generation Number Plates

Keltech Team

Published by Keltech Online Cyber Services

In a sweeping directive issued on May 21, the Kenyan Government has mandated that all public institutions replace their vehicle number plates with the new generation plates by August 29, 2025.

What You Need to Know

The National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) has been tasked with overseeing this transition to enhance vehicle identification and security across the country. The new generation number plates feature advanced security features including:

  • Enhanced reflective materials for better visibility
  • Embedded security chips to prevent counterfeiting
  • Standardized font and sizing across all plates
  • QR codes for quick verification

All government vehicles must have the new plates installed by August 29, 2025. Failure to comply may result in penalties and vehicle grounding.

How to Get Your New Number Plates

The process of obtaining new generation number plates has been simplified through the NTSA TIMS system:

  1. Log in to NTSA TIMS - Visit the official NTSA portal at tims.ntsa.go.ke
  2. Select Number Plate Replacement - Navigate to the vehicle services section
  3. Submit Required Documents - Upload your logbook and identification documents
  4. Make Payment - Pay the prescribed fee via M-Pesa or bank transfer
  5. Book Collection Appointment - Choose your preferred NTSA office for plate collection

Cost and Requirements

The cost for new generation number plates varies depending on the vehicle category:

Why the Change?

The government's decision to mandate new generation number plates is part of a broader strategy to modernize vehicle registration and enhance road safety. The new plates will help in:

  • Reducing vehicle theft through better tracking
  • Improving law enforcement capabilities
  • Facilitating automated toll collection systems
  • Enhancing border security and customs clearance
  • Creating a centralized vehicle database

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What happens if I miss the deadline?

A: Vehicles without compliant plates after the deadline may face penalties, impoundment, or restricted access to certain areas.

Q: Can I keep my old number?

A: Yes, the new generation plates will retain your existing registration number, only the physical plate design changes.

Q: How long does the process take?

A: From application to collection, the process typically takes 3-5 working days, depending on the NTSA office.
